### ADR-087 — Token counting: official provider counting first, calibrated byte heuristic as fallback

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Context Manager, Indexing Engine, Provider Layer
- Related requirements: FR-CTX-002, FR-IDX-002; NFR-CTX-001

#### Context

Token budgeting (FR-CTX-002) and chunk sizing (FR-IDX-002) need per-content token costs.
Volume 3 fixes the port surface: `ProviderPort.CountTokens` returns counts exactly when the
provider offers an official counting mechanism and otherwise returns the capability-
unavailable error class "so the Context Manager falls back to its estimation strategy
(Volume 7)" — this ADR is that strategy. Tokenization is model-family-specific; exact local
tokenizers exist for some families but their availability, licensing, and fidelity per model
are external facts this specification must not assert (assumptions policy, Volume 0).

#### Problem

What token-cost source does Volume 7 use per model, and what happens when no official count
is available — without inventing tokenizer capabilities, without model-name special-casing
(Principle 2), and without context-window overflows becoming routine?

#### Forces and constraints

- Principle 2: behavior keys off declared capabilities, never model names.
- Overflow costs a failed provider round-trip (NFR-CTX-001 targets zero); undercounting is
  therefore worse than overcounting.
- Estimation must be deterministic and cheap (hot path, SM-12 replay).
- Bundling third-party tokenizers has licensing/size costs and still covers only some
  families.

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Bundle local tokenizers per model family** — deferred, PENDING VALIDATION (recorded in
   the Volume 7 register): availability, licenses, and per-model fidelity are unverified
   external facts; adoption would be per-family, additive, and behind the same counting
   interface.
2. **Always estimate, never call CountTokens** — rejected: discards exact counts that are
   officially available, inflating waste of budget headroom.
3. **Official counting when declared, calibrated heuristic otherwise** (selected).

#### Decision

Token costs resolve in fixed order: (1) `ProviderPort.CountTokens` when the provider
declares an official counting mechanism, cached by `(model, content_hash)`; (2) otherwise
the deterministic heuristic: `estimated_tokens = ceil(utf8_bytes / 4) + 8` per item (8 =
fixed per-item structural overhead), with an inflation factor of 1.15 applied to estimates
only. Budgets additionally reserve a safety margin (`context.budget.safety_margin_ratio`,
default 5% of the window). Estimates are always marked `estimated = true` in assembly
metadata. The divisor, overhead, and inflation constants are fixed by this ADR (not
user-configurable) so that replay and fixtures are stable; the safety margin is
configuration.

#### Rationale

The bytes/4 ratio is a deliberately conservative engineering constant for the mixed
code-and-prose content Andromeda handles; correctness does not depend on its precision
because the inflation factor and safety margin absorb error in the safe direction
(overcounting excludes a marginal candidate; undercounting causes provider rejections).
Marking estimates keeps honesty: users and tests can distinguish measured from estimated
budgets.

#### Positive consequences

- Zero provider round-trips wasted on counting where unsupported; exact counts used where
  official.
- Deterministic, dependency-free fallback; identical on all platforms.
- NFR-CTX-001's overflow target is achievable even for estimation-path models.

#### Negative consequences

- Overcounting wastes budget headroom on estimation-path models (bounded by the 1.15
  factor plus margin).
- The per-item overhead constant is a simplification of provider-specific message framing
  costs.

#### Risks

- A model family whose true tokens-per-byte ratio exceeds the inflated estimate would still
  overflow — detected by NFR-CTX-001 measurement classifying provider overflow errors, and
  bounded by the safety margin.

#### Reversal plan

The cost source sits behind one internal costing interface keyed by capability. Adopting a
validated local tokenizer (alternative 1) adds a third source between official counting and
the heuristic, per model family, with no consumer changes. Constants can be recalibrated by
a superseding ADR with fixture updates.

#### Review conditions

- NFR-CTX-001 measurements show overflow on estimation-path models, or waste (unused budget
  from overestimation) exceeding 15% median.
- The PENDING VALIDATION on local tokenizer adoption resolves for a family in wide use.
