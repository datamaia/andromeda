### ADR-041 — Plan representation: versioned task DAG with supersession

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Planner, Execution Engine, Core Domain, Persistence Layer
- Related requirements: FR-AGT-007, FR-AGT-008; PRD-005, PRD-006

#### Context

Volume 2 defines Plan and Task entities with per-run plan versions, dense ordinals, explicit
dependencies, and the invariant set INV-PLAN-01..04 / INV-TASK-01..05. Volume 4 must fix the
concrete representation the Planner produces and the Execution Engine consumes, including
how plans change mid-run and how trivial goals are handled.

#### Problem

What is the normative shape of a plan — its structure, its granularity of change, and its
treatment of goals too small to decompose?

#### Forces and constraints

- Plans must be inspectable and approvable before side effects (PRD-005) and auditable
  afterward (PRD-006).
- Mid-run change is the norm, not the exception; lineage must survive it.
- Dependency expressiveness must cover parallelism (Beta) without a workflow language
  (workflows are the Workflow Engine's job, FR-WF-001).
- Trivial goals must not force decomposition theater.

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Free-form plan text (a model-maintained todo list)** — rejected: not machine-checkable,
   no dependency semantics, approval gates would gate prose, divergence undetectable.
2. **Hierarchical task trees with nested sub-plans** — rejected: nesting complicates the
   ready computation, state reporting, and the TUI; delegation (ADR-045) covers hierarchy at
   the agent level instead.
3. **In-place plan mutation with an edit log** — rejected: "what was approved" becomes a
   reconstruction exercise; versioned supersession keeps every approved artifact immutable.
4. **Flat task DAG per plan version, whole-version supersession** — chosen.

#### Decision

A plan version is a flat, ordinal-ordered list of Tasks with explicit same-plan
dependencies forming a DAG. Change happens by whole-version supersession: the predecessor
becomes `superseded` and a successor version (remaining and new work only) is persisted in
one transaction (FR-AGT-008). Goals classified single-step produce a one-task
direct-execution plan.

#### Rationale

Flat DAGs give the Execution Engine a tractable ready computation and users a renderable
plan; whole-version supersession gives approval gates immutable subjects and complete
lineage; direct-execution plans keep the every-run-has-a-plan invariant (ADR-040) at near
zero cost.

#### Positive consequences

- Deterministic validation (acyclicity, density) is cheap and code-enforced.
- Approval artifacts are immutable; lineage answers "what changed and why".
- One record shape for every run size.

#### Negative consequences

- Large re-plans rewrite many task rows (bounded by `max_tasks_per_plan`).
- No intra-plan hierarchy; deeply structured work needs workflows or delegation.

#### Risks

- Version churn from over-eager revision — bounded by `agent.planner.max_revisions`.
- Task granularity chosen poorly by models — mitigated by validation bounds and revision.

#### Reversal plan

Hierarchy could be added additively (a parent-task field) under the change procedure;
supersession semantics and persisted lineage remain valid under such an extension.

#### Review conditions

Review if workflow-engine experience (Volume 4 chapters 06–09) shows systematic need for
intra-plan hierarchy, or if revision-bound cancellations are frequent in field metrics.
