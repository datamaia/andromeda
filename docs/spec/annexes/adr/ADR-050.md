### ADR-050 — Workflow resume at persisted step boundaries only, with approval-gated re-execution of side-effecting steps

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team (Volume 4 authoring)
- Components: Workflow Engine, Runtime (recovery), Permission Manager, Persistence Layer
- Related requirements: FR-WF-006; NFR-WF-002

#### Context

Workflow Runs are long-lived and will meet crashes and shutdowns (PRD-010). Volume 2 fixes
step-boundary persistence (INV-WFR-03) and the `interrupted` semantics; Volume 3 chapter 08
(RISK-ARCH-004) records that the gap between persisted state and actual side effects at a
kill point cannot be fully closed. Volume 4 must decide the resume granularity and what
happens to steps caught mid-flight.

#### Problem

At what granularity do interrupted workflow runs resume, and under what rule may a step that
was executing at the crash run again, given that its side effects may have partially
occurred?

#### Forces and constraints

- Mid-step checkpointing would require checkpointing spawned Runs' arbitrary tool effects —
  unimplementable honestly for external effects.
- Silently re-executing a side-effecting step risks duplicate effects (double commits,
  double writes).
- Never re-executing anything strands runs permanently on any crash.
- The human is the only agent that can judge ambiguous partial effects (PRD-005).

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Mid-step checkpoint and continue** — rejected: claims a precision the system cannot
   have for effects outside its transaction boundary; RISK-ARCH-004 explicitly warns against
   guessing across this gap.
2. **Automatic re-execution of interrupted steps** — rejected for side-effecting steps:
   duplicate side effects are worse than a paused run; acceptable only where effects are
   provably absent.
3. **Manual-only recovery (user re-runs the whole workflow)** — rejected: discards the value
   of step-boundary persistence and punishes users for crashes they did not cause.

#### Decision

Interrupted Workflow Runs resume **only at persisted step boundaries**. Completed steps and
granted gates never re-execute (INV-WFR-04). A step recorded `interrupted` re-dispatches
automatically only when its declared `effects` is `none`; steps with `workspace` or
`external` effects re-dispatch only after an explicit **resume Approval** that presents the
step's recorded partial effects. Inconsistent `step_states` fails the run (E-WF-012) rather
than executing on unreliable state.

#### Rationale

The design tells the truth about what it knows: boundaries are known-consistent points;
effect classes are declared facts; humans arbitrate the genuinely ambiguous cases. This is
Volume 3's "refuse to guess across the gap" made concrete.

#### Positive consequences

- Zero silent duplicate side effects by construction (NFR-WF-002's blocking criterion).
- Resume behavior is predictable from the definition alone.
- Read-only pipelines recover with no human involvement.

#### Negative consequences

- Long side-effecting steps lose more work on crash than mid-step checkpointing would;
  authors mitigate by decomposing long stages (SDD's implementation stage spawns per-wave
  runs).
- Resume of side-effecting steps requires a human, limiting unattended recovery.

#### Risks

- `effects` misdeclaration (`none` on a mutating step) re-introduces silent re-execution;
  mitigation: the Tool Runtime records actual side effects, and a `none`-declared step that
  produced File Changes or Command Executions is flagged as a definition defect at run time.
- Approval fatigue on crash-prone setups; mitigation: the Approval presents concrete
  recorded effects, and crash recurrence is itself surfaced.

#### Reversal plan

Finer-grained resume could be added per step kind behind the same rules (a step kind that
proves checkpointable declares it); the boundary-resume contract remains the floor, so
loosening is additive.

#### Review conditions

- Re-examine if crash-injection data (SM-11 methodology) shows > 5% of resumes stranded on
  resume Approvals users reflexively grant.
- Re-examine when sandbox-level effect tracking (Volume 9 Beta/v1 layers) makes partial
  effects mechanically enumerable.
