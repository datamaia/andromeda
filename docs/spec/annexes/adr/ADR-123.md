### ADR-123 — Audit chain verification: O(1) head check by default, full walk on demand

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Audit Log, Persistence Layer, CLI (doctor, export)
- Related requirements: FR-SEC-111, NFR-SEC-005

#### Context

Volume 2 fixes the Audit Record's tamper-evidence mechanism: per-database SHA-256 hash
chains with `prev_hash`/`record_hash` and a zero-hash sentinel (INV-AUD-02), and delegates
the verification procedure, tamper response, and retention to Volume 9. Audit databases grow
to hundreds of thousands of records over a workspace's life; database open sits on the
startup path with Volume 12 cold-start budgets.

#### Problem

When is the chain verified, at what cost, and what happens — concretely, for a local
single-user product with no external trust anchor — when verification fails?

#### Forces and constraints

- Startup budgets (SM-06 formalization in Volume 12) forbid O(n) work on open.
- Tamper evidence is only valuable if verification actually runs; a verify-never default
  would make the chain decorative.
- A local SQLite file offers no way to prove *who* tampered — honesty about what the
  mechanism proves is a specification duty (Volume 0 assumptions policy).
- Evidence preservation outranks convenience (precedence order: integrity before UX).
- Verification must be possible offline, including on exported archives (PRD-003).

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Full verification on every open** — rejected: O(n) on the startup path; a 100k-record
   chain costs seconds (NFR-SEC-005 measurement), blowing cold-start budgets for a check
   whose failure mode is rare.
2. **No default verification (doctor-only)** — rejected: tampering or corruption would sit
   undetected until a user happens to run diagnostics; the chain's deterrence value
   collapses.
3. **External anchoring (publishing chain heads to a remote log or signing with a machine
   key)** — rejected for now: contradicts local-first defaults (no phone-home without
   consent), and a locally stored signing key adds no proof against a local attacker who
   can also use it; recorded as a review condition for enterprise policy needs.
4. **Head check on open + full verification at defined trigger points** (chosen).

#### Decision

Verification runs at two granularities: an O(1) **head check** (recompute the newest
record's hash and its link) at every database open, default-on via
`security.audit_verify_on_open = "head"`; and **full chain verification** by `andromeda
doctor`, the scheduled verification task, before every audit export, and after every restore
from backup. Divergence freezes the database for normal writes, preserves and reports every
consistent segment, opens an incident, and requires recorded acknowledgment to re-anchor a
new chain segment referencing the break and the evidence-export digest. Retention archives
JSONL (offline re-verifiable) before any post-window pruning.

#### Rationale

The head check catches the cheap failure classes (truncation, last-write corruption) at
constant cost; the trigger points put full verification exactly where its answer matters
(diagnostics, evidence export, post-restore trust). Freeze-and-acknowledge is the honest
response for a mechanism that proves *that* history changed, not *who* changed it: the
product neither destroys evidence nor pretends certainty it cannot have.

#### Positive consequences

- Tamper evidence with zero measurable startup cost (≤ 5 ms head check, NFR-SEC-005).
- Exports are self-contained evidence: any SHA-256 implementation re-verifies them.
- Restore-from-backup gets a defined trust ritual instead of silent acceptance.

#### Negative consequences

- Mid-chain tampering between full verifications goes undetected until the next trigger
  point — bounded by the scheduled task's cadence.
- Freeze-on-divergence interrupts work until acknowledgment; deliberate, but user-visible.

#### Risks

- Users acknowledging breaks reflexively, eroding the ritual; mitigated by requiring the
  evidence export to complete first (the acknowledgment references its digest).
- Storage-level corruption misread as tampering; mitigated by running ADR-029 integrity
  checks alongside and reporting the distinction where SQLite-level checks also fail.

#### Reversal plan

Verification triggers and responses are Audit Log-internal policy; strengthening (e.g.,
adding external anchoring behind consent) is additive. The chain format itself is Volume 2's
and untouched by this decision.

#### Review conditions

- If enterprise deployments (Future phase policy distribution) need third-party-verifiable
  logs, revisit external anchoring behind explicit consent.
- If NFR-SEC-005 measurements show full verification ≤ 1 s at the reference population,
  consider promoting full verification to scheduled-daily default.
