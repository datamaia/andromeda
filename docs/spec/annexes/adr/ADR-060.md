### ADR-060 — Explicit-first routing and guarded, configuration-only fallback chains

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Provider Router, Permission Manager, Policy Engine, CLI/TUI
- Related requirements: FR-PROV-042, FR-PROV-043, FR-PROV-013

#### Context

Volume 3 fixes the composite shape: the Provider Router implements ProviderPort, so
consumers cannot know which provider serves them except through announced attribution.
Principle 1 requires selection, routing, and fallback to be user- and policy-controlled with
every change reported; the brief mandates rules preventing unsafe or costly fallback.
Fallback crosses trust boundaries: cost profiles, data jurisdictions, capability sets.

#### Problem

How autonomous is provider selection: fully automatic optimization, no automation at all,
or explicit selection with narrowly scoped, user-configured automation — and what makes a
fallback step legal?

#### Forces and constraints

- Principle 1/7: no silent provider or model changes, ever.
- Local→cloud fallback is data egress — a security decision, not a resilience tweak.
- Headless runs must resolve decisions from policy without hanging or silently granting
  (PRD-009).
- Cost surprises destroy trust faster than downtime; unknown pricing must not block
  everything nor permit everything.
- Determinism is required for run reproducibility (SM-12).

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Automatic cost/latency-optimizing routing by default** — rejected for MVP: opaque
   selection is the opposite of the announced-attribution model; optimization without
   dependable pricing data (ADR-058) would optimize noise.
2. **No fallback at all** — rejected: local-model and multi-provider setups reasonably want
   continuity; refusing all automation pushes users to scripts that hide switches.
3. **Implicit fallback to "any capable provider"** — rejected: violates the egress and cost
   guards by construction.
4. **Explicit-first routing plus configuration-only chains with ordered guard rules** —
   chosen.

#### Decision

Routing strategies at MVP are exactly `explicit` (named provider+model used verbatim) and
`preference_list` (ordered configured candidates), with the capability filter and
state/breaker/policy exclusions always applied and every delegated selection evented and
announced. Fallback exists only through configured chains evaluated under the ordered guard
rules F1–F8 of Volume 5, chapter 05: explicit chains only; local→cloud egress requires
opt-in plus a `network` grant; capability and policy guards; price-multiplier cost guard
with approval; no mid-stream switches; no default fallback on authentication failures;
mandatory announcement before the target's first response. Scored automatic routing is a
Beta candidate under the same guard regime.

#### Rationale

Explicit-first keeps the human in control of vendor exposure; chains encode intent once,
under guards that make the dangerous cases (silent egress, runaway cost, capability lies)
structurally impossible rather than merely discouraged.

#### Positive consequences

- Every provider/model change is attributable, announced, and reproducible.
- Fallback misconfiguration fails toward refusal with recorded reasons (E-PROV-016).
- Security review of fallback reduces to reviewing eight ordered guards.

#### Negative consequences

- Continuity requires configuration; out-of-the-box behavior is fail-with-reasons.
- Guard evaluation adds decision latency at failure time (local, bounded).

#### Risks

- Habitual approval of cost prompts erodes the guard; mitigation: scoped approvals recorded
  per Volume 9, visible spend per attempt (RISK-PROV-004).

#### Reversal plan

Strategies and guards are router policy: adding scored strategies or relaxing a guard is a
configuration-surface change through the Volume 0 procedure; removing the announcement duty
would contradict Principle 1/7 and is not reversible below a corpus-level decision.

#### Review conditions

- At Beta: evaluate scored routing against real pricing-table adoption and SM-12 replay
  results.
- Review guard order if a real incident shows a cheaper-to-evaluate guard should run
  earlier.
