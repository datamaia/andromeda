### ADR-086 — Deterministic tiered context assembly with refusal-over-drop for mandatory content

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Context Manager, Agent Engine, Prompt Engine
- Related requirements: FR-CTX-001, FR-CTX-002, FR-CTX-005; NFR-CTX-001

#### Context

Every model request needs a context set selected from seven source kinds (Volume 2's frozen
Context Item vocabulary) under a hard token budget. Volume 1 commits to reproducibility
(SM-12) and transparency (PRD-006): the corpus must be able to say exactly what went to the
model and why. Selection strategies range from pure scoring (one global ranked list) to
learned or model-assisted selection.

#### Problem

How are candidates ordered into a bounded budget such that (a) results are deterministic and
replayable, (b) instruction-critical content can never be silently displaced by
merely-relevant content, and (c) the pipeline adds no inference cost or latency of its own?

#### Forces and constraints

- SM-12 replay requires bit-identical assembly from identical inputs — no randomness, no
  model-in-the-loop selection.
- System prompts, pins, and the current intent are qualitatively different from retrieved
  candidates: dropping them changes what the agent *is*, not just what it knows.
- Small local models have small windows; the strategy must degrade predictably there
  (PRD-003 local-first).
- Assembly sits on the per-turn hot path; its own cost must be bounded and local.

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Single global score with weighted factors** — rejected: a sufficiently high-scoring
   retrieval hit could displace the system prompt or a pin; guaranteeing it cannot requires
   infinite weights, which is tiering by another name, less honestly.
2. **Model-assisted selection** (ask a cheap model what matters) — rejected for the
   pipeline: nondeterministic, costs latency/money per turn, and creates a circular
   dependency (context needed to select context); revisit only as an offline advisor.
3. **Fixed priority tiers with greedy scored fill and refusal on mandatory overflow**
   (selected).

#### Decision

Assembly uses eight fixed priority tiers (system/skill prompts; pins; current intent;
history; tool results; memory; index hits; extra files). Tiers 1–3 are mandatory: if they
alone exceed the budget, assembly refuses (E-CTX-001) rather than dropping any of them.
Within a tier, candidates order by score descending, tie-broken by ULID ascending. Every
included and excluded candidate is recorded as a Context Item. The pipeline performs no
inference and no network I/O.

#### Rationale

Tiers encode product judgments (instructions outrank recall) structurally, where they are
auditable and testable; determinism comes free from fixed order plus total tie-breaks; the
refusal rule converts the worst silent failure (an agent that lost its instructions) into an
explicit, actionable error.

#### Positive consequences

- Replayable assemblies; golden-fixture testability (Volume 13).
- RISK-CTX-001's worst case is structurally excluded for mandatory content.
- No added inference cost or provider dependence in the hot path.

#### Negative consequences

- Greedy per-tier fill is not globally optimal packing; some budget slack survives (bounded
  by item granularity).
- Fixed tiers cannot express "this one memory outranks old history" without a pin —
  the user override exists for exactly that.

#### Risks

- Tier order itself could be wrong for some workflows — mitigated by exclusion/pinning
  controls (FR-CTX-005) and recorded exclusions enabling evidence-based revision.

#### Reversal plan

The pipeline is internal to the Context Manager behind its assembly API: a smarter packer or
re-tiering replaces the budgeting stage without touching Context Item recording, ports, or
consumers. Recorded snapshots (FR-CTX-007) make A/B evaluation of a successor offline
possible before any swap.

#### Review conditions

- Exclusion-record analysis shows systematic quality loss attributable to tier order.
- A future offline context-advisor capability (post-v1 candidate) matures enough to justify
  revisiting alternative 2 outside the hot path.
