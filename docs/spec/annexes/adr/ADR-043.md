### ADR-043 — Conservative resumption of interrupted work

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Agent Engine, Execution Engine, Runtime, Permission Manager
- Related requirements: FR-AGT-003; FR-ARCH-009; PRD-010

#### Context

Crash recovery (FR-ARCH-009) marks orphaned runs and tasks `interrupted` and offers them as
resumable (UC-11). The gap between recorded and actual state at a kill point is not fully
closable (RISK-ARCH-004): a tool killed mid-write may or may not have landed its effect.
Volume 4 must fix what resumption actually does with interrupted work.

#### Problem

Which interrupted work restarts automatically on resume, which requires human confirmation,
and what happens to in-flight turns and expired approvals?

#### Forces and constraints

- PRD-010: interrupted work is never assumed complete — and equally never assumed absent.
- Silent re-execution of side-effecting work risks duplication; silent skipping risks
  half-done work presented as done.
- Read-only work is safe to redo by definition.
- Approvals have expiries (Volume 9); resumption must not resurrect stale consent.
- Resume friction must stay low enough that users actually resume (UC-11 value).

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Restart everything interrupted** — rejected: re-executes side-effecting work whose
   effects may have landed; the duplication failure mode again.
2. **Restart nothing; require the user to re-drive each task** — rejected: makes resumption
   barely better than a new run; punishes the common safe case (read-only work).
3. **Model-decided resumption** (ask the agent what is safe) — rejected: delegates an
   integrity decision to the component with the least reliable judgment and no liability.
4. **Classification by recorded side effects: auto-resume effect-free work, confirm the
   rest** — chosen.

#### Decision

On resume: the in-progress turn at the stop point is recorded `interrupted` and never
replayed; completed and skipped tasks stand; interrupted tasks with zero recorded side
effects in their current attempt re-enter `ready` automatically (attempt incremented);
interrupted tasks with recorded side effects require explicit user confirmation, presented
with the recorded effects and the workspace snapshot delta; expired Approvals are
re-requested, never honored; non-interactive resumption treats required confirmations as
denials (PRD-009 rule — no hidden prompts).

#### Rationale

The same persisted facts that gate retries (ADR-042) gate resumption, giving one auditable
integrity rule across both boundaries; the classification puts the human exactly where
ambiguity is irreducible and nowhere else.

#### Positive consequences

- Zero silent re-execution of side-effecting work (NFR-AGT-002 identity property).
- Cheap resumes for the common read-only-interruption case.
- One mental model for retry and resume gating.

#### Negative consequences

- Confirmations add friction after crashes involving effectful work.
- Truly idempotent effectful tasks pay an unnecessary confirmation.

#### Risks

- Users rubber-stamping confirmations — mitigated by presenting recorded effects and deltas,
  not bare yes/no prompts (Volume 8 wireframes).

#### Reversal plan

The classification is the ADR-042 classifier applied at resume; loosening follows the same
path as loosening retries, with this ADR superseded.

#### Review conditions

Review alongside ADR-042's idempotency condition; review if SM-11 field data shows
confirmation-heavy resumes deterring session recovery.
