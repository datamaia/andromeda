### ADR-162 — Closed, evented degraded-mode catalog; no silent degradation

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Runtime, Provider Layer, Indexing Engine, Context Manager, Secret Store, Sandbox Engine, TUI, CLI, Observability
- Related requirements: Volume 12 FR-PERF-003, NFR-PERF-024, NFR-PERF-028

#### Context

Andromeda runs on developer machines where partial failure is the normal case: airplanes
(offline), full disks, absent keychains, stale indexes, rate-limited providers, missing
OS-isolation mechanisms (ADR-021). Volume 1 makes local-first operation contractual
(PRD-003) and Volume 5 already mandates user notification on provider/model changes. What
was undecided is *how* the runtime represents reduced service generally: ad-hoc per-feature
fallbacks, or a governed mechanism.

#### Problem

How does the runtime represent, expose, and test reduced-service conditions so that
degradation is predictable, observable, and never mistaken for either full service or total
failure?

#### Forces and constraints

- Users must be able to trust what the tool says it is doing (PRD-006); silent fallback to
  weaker behavior — weaker retrieval, weaker credential storage, weaker isolation — breaks
  that trust and can be security-relevant.
- Per-feature ad-hoc fallbacks multiply untested states (the degradation-matrix risk).
- Security-sensitive degradations (secret fallback, sandbox downgrade) have policy owned by
  Volume 9; performance/reliability needs the *mechanism* without re-owning the policy.
- Automation (headless, CI) needs machine-readable degradation signals, not TUI banners.

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Ad-hoc per-feature fallbacks** (status quo of most tools) — rejected: untestable state
   space, invisible to automation, and exactly how silent security downgrades happen.
2. **A global binary "degraded" flag** — rejected: loses which capability is reduced;
   forces worst-case assumptions everywhere; useless for targeted UX.
3. **Persisting degraded modes as entity state** — rejected: modes are environmental
   conditions, not domain state; persistence would let stale degradation survive the
   conditions that caused it (recovery must re-evaluate from scratch).

#### Decision

- A **closed catalog** of eight degraded modes (Volume 12, FR-PERF-003): `offline`,
  `provider_limited`, `index_unavailable`, `no_embeddings`, `secret_fallback`, `low_disk`,
  `low_memory`, `sandbox_reduced` — each with trigger, constrained behavior, visibility,
  exit condition with hysteresis, and phase.
- One uniform lifecycle: atomic entry with `perf.degradation.entered`, queryable while
  active, evented exit with `perf.degradation.exited`; modes compose independently; modes
  are never persisted across restart.
- **No silent degradation anywhere:** behavior reduced without its event and indicator is a
  defect corpus-wide. Adding a mode amends FR-PERF-003 and its conformance suite together.
- Policy content of security-relevant modes stays with Volume 9; Volume 5 keeps provider
  routing semantics; this ADR fixes the representation and observability mechanism.

#### Rationale

A closed, named catalog turns "it behaves weirdly sometimes" into an enumerable, testable
state space with a uniform contract, at the cost of a governance step when a genuinely new
mode appears — the right trade for a tool whose core promises are transparency and
local-first operation. Events plus queryable status serve TUI, CLI, and automation with one
mechanism.

#### Positive consequences

- Degradation is testable per mode and pairwise, with an event-pairing audit as a cheap
  invariant.
- The offline promise becomes a designed mode with a conformance suite, not an error path.
- Security downgrades are structurally incapable of being silent.

#### Negative consequences

- New degradation scenarios require a specification change, not just code.
- Eight modes with hysteresis and composition are real implementation surface.
- Indicator and status plumbing touches every driver surface.

#### Risks

- Catalog incompleteness pushing real conditions into generic errors (mitigation: field
  diagnostics review at phase gates; the change procedure adds modes deliberately);
  mode-composition bugs (mitigation: pairwise fixtures plus randomized composition in the
  soak, RISK-PERF-003).

#### Reversal plan

The catalog and lifecycle live in FR-PERF-003 and one runtime component; collapsing to
fewer modes or a different representation supersedes this ADR and deprecates affected mode
identifiers through the change procedure, keeping the no-silent-degradation invariant,
which is independently required by PRD-006-derived requirements.

#### Review conditions

- Field diagnostics show recurring reduced-service conditions not representable in the
  catalog.
- Volume 9's sandbox/secret policies evolve in ways the mode surface cannot express.
- The pairwise test strategy proves insufficient (composition defects reaching releases).
