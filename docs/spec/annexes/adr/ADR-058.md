### ADR-058 — No shipped price data: cost estimates come from user-maintained pricing tables

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-11
- Deciders: Andromeda specification team
- Components: Provider Layer, Configuration Manager, Runtime (Cost Records), CLI/TUI
- Related requirements: FR-PROV-030, FR-PROV-031

#### Context

Cost transparency is a product commitment (Principle 7: estimated cost, and actual cost when
determinable). Volume 2 fixes the Cost Record with `cost_basis` ∈ `actual` | `estimated` |
`unavailable`, integer micro-unit money (INV-COST-02), and honesty rules (INV-COST-01/03).
Few providers officially report per-request monetary cost; estimates require per-token
prices. Provider prices change at vendors' discretion, differ by account tier, and the
project's assumptions policy prohibits asserting unverified commercial facts (prices are on
the never-invent list).

#### Problem

Where do per-token prices come from: shipped in Andromeda releases, fetched from providers,
or supplied by users — such that estimates are possible without the project asserting
commercial facts it cannot keep true?

#### Forces and constraints

- Assumptions policy: never invent prices or commercial conditions.
- Releases are point-in-time; prices are not. A shipped table is wrong the day after.
- No documented, cross-provider price API exists as a dependable product surface.
- INV-COST-03: estimates must never present as actuals.
- Local-first: cost computation must work offline.

#### Alternatives considered

1. **Ship a maintained price catalog in releases** — rejected: asserts external commercial
   facts with release-cadence staleness; wrong numbers with product authority are worse
   than absent numbers.
2. **Scrape or fetch provider pricing pages at runtime** — rejected: no documented API
   contract; violates the official-mechanisms constraint and offline operation.
3. **User-maintained pricing tables in configuration, with provenance fields** — chosen.

#### Decision

Andromeda ships no price data. Monetary cost resolves: `actual` from official
`cost_reporting` only; `estimated` from user-maintained
`[providers.<slug>.pricing."<model>"]` tables with required `source` and `effective_date`
fields (integer micro-units per million tokens, ISO 4217 currency); `unavailable`
otherwise. Estimates are labeled everywhere they surface; mixed-basis aggregates are
flagged; no implicit currency conversion exists.

#### Rationale

The user is the only party who knows their negotiated prices and tier; provenance fields
make estimate quality inspectable; the honesty labels shift the product promise from "we
know prices" to "we account truthfully with what is known" — which is the Principle 7
commitment as written.

#### Positive consequences

- Zero invented commercial facts in releases; no staleness liability.
- Offline-capable estimation; account-tier-accurate when users maintain tables.
- `actual`/`estimated`/`unavailable` distribution becomes a useful telemetry signal.

#### Negative consequences

- Out-of-the-box installs show `unavailable` cost until tables are configured or providers
  report cost.
- Table maintenance is a user chore (RISK-PROV-003).

#### Risks

- Stale tables mislead; mitigation: mandatory `effective_date` shown in cost views,
  divergence surfacing where reported and estimated coexist (RISK-PROV-003).

#### Reversal plan

If a documented, official cross-provider pricing surface emerges, an importer can populate
the same table shape from it under a new ADR; the resolution rules and record schema are
unchanged — reversal is additive.

#### Review conditions

- Review when any seed provider ships documented programmatic pricing.
- Review if telemetry (consenting installs) shows `unavailable` dominating long-term —
  a signal the chore is too heavy and an import path deserves priority.
